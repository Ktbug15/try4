Finding My Passion

When I was younger, I saw all my peers who all had their "thing". I had spent most of my childhood trying so many different sports but I never seemed to excel at any of them. Basketball was the sport I clung to the most. In fact, I spent hours and hours alone trying to improve my skill. I gave 100% every practice but to no avail. I was benched almost every game. After going through this cycle again and again seeing no fruit of my labor, I decided to put my efforts into something else, Tennis.

Fulfilling My Potential

When I started playing Tennis, I could barely consider myself coordinated. My shots went everywhere, my grip was extremely unnatural, and my footwork and ball placement was worthy of a laugh. But seeing what potential there was, I locked in and started practicing. I remained committed from day 1 that I was going to work as hard as I could because when I played, I fell in love. Over time and through diligent practice, I was able to place #3 Singles for my High School Tennis Team. I had finally made it. There was nothing like it.

Overcoming Setbacks

As with any success story, there are setbacks. The summer before my senior year, I was in a serious car accident which left me broken, on the outside and inside. My right wrist was broken, and I had bruises all over my entire body. I had torn nerves in my knee causing irreprable damage. I was devastated. I had worked so hard to get to where I was and I was planning to practice all summer to be ready for my last season of tennis and it seemed all was lost.

Well, 2 weeks after my surgery, I got back on the court. Unable to run without getting dizzy, I walked around trying to be a part of all the drills and playing with my left hand. I did what I could while knowing my likelyhood of playing varsity singles was slim. But I came to practice every morning of the summer, learning how to play all over again. Tryouts came two weeks after I got my cast off and I was praying for one thing, that I would just be able to play--and play without pain.

A Story to Tell

Tryouts came and I witnessed a miracle. I was playing against a varsity level player, and as I swung, I could actually hit the ball! Though I lost that match, I was blown away that I could hit the ball with my arm, use power, and feel no pain. The fire was back. Well, I played for the rest of tryouts feeling somewhat confident in my abilities and when the roster came out, I got the very last spot on varsity. I was absolutely thrilled. I knew it was divine intervention. As the season progressed, my partner and I kept winning match after match after match. And the time came for the region championship, and we won. This victory to me would not have been nearly as significant. I was shown that comebacks are more rewarding than just regular old success.

Coaching

Being mentored by a coach who believed in me changed my life. Unlike in basketball, where my coaches picked their favorites and only played to win, my tennis coach let everyone have an opportunity to play. He gave everyone a chance to improve. He saw our potential. I have since taken up coaching. I cherish the opportunity to watch these young tennis players grow. I get to help them see the potential I see in them. I truly believe each kid I coach has the potential to be incredible because I remember how "skilled" I was when I started. Effort and belief can accomplish incredible things
